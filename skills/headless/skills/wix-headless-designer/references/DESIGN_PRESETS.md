# Design Presets — Inspiration Reference

These are starting points for creative direction — NOT templates to copy verbatim. Every site must have a unique identity. Use these to understand what a direction feels like, then make it your own.

## Aesthetic Directions

### Organic / Natural
**Feel:** Warm, grounded, tactile
**Typography:** Serif display (Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond) + humanist sans body (Source Sans 3, Nunito)
**Palette:** Warm cream, forest green, terracotta, muted gold
**Layout:** Generous whitespace, asymmetric image placement, soft curves
**Motion:** Slow, gentle fades. Parallax on nature imagery.
**Good for:** Wellness, skincare, food, home goods, sustainable brands

### Luxury / Refined
**Feel:** Exclusive, restrained, high-end
**Typography:** Elegant serif (Cormorant, EB Garamond) or sharp sans (Outfit, Syne) + refined body text
**Palette:** Deep black, warm white, single metallic accent (gold, rose gold)
**Layout:** Extreme whitespace, oversized typography, full-bleed imagery
**Motion:** Smooth, deliberate transitions. Subtle text reveals.
**Good for:** Fashion, jewelry, high-end retail, professional services

### Editorial / Magazine
**Feel:** Curated, story-driven, sophisticated
**Typography:** Strong contrast between display and body (e.g., Fraunces + DM Sans, or Newsreader + Work Sans)
**Palette:** High contrast — near-black and near-white with one accent color
**Layout:** Column-based, text-heavy with dramatic image placement, pull quotes
**Motion:** Content fading in as you scroll, staggered reveals
**Good for:** Blog-focused sites, portfolios, content-driven brands

### Brutalist / Raw
**Feel:** Bold, unapologetic, unconventional
**Typography:** Heavy weights, monospace accents (Space Mono, JetBrains Mono, Archivo Black)
**Palette:** High contrast — black/white with neon accent, or unexpected combinations
**Layout:** Dense, grid-breaking, overlapping elements, visible borders
**Motion:** Abrupt, glitchy, or deliberately none
**Good for:** Creative agencies, tech startups, art, music

### Soft / Pastel
**Feel:** Friendly, approachable, calming
**Typography:** Rounded sans (Nunito, Quicksand, Comfortaa) + clean body text
**Palette:** Soft pastels — lavender, mint, peach, baby blue — with a deeper anchor color
**Layout:** Rounded corners, card-based, generous padding, playful arrangement
**Motion:** Bouncy, springy animations. Playful hover effects.
**Good for:** Children's brands, wellness apps, friendly SaaS, community platforms

### Retro-Futuristic
**Feel:** Nostalgic yet forward-looking, fun
**Typography:** Geometric display (Syne, Orbitron, Clash Display) + clean sans body
**Palette:** Deep purple/navy + electric accents (cyan, magenta, neon green)
**Layout:** Grid-heavy, geometric shapes, angular dividers, gradient overlays
**Motion:** Smooth transitions with cubic-bezier curves, hover transforms
**Good for:** Tech products, gaming, creative studios, music

### Industrial / Utilitarian
**Feel:** Functional, no-nonsense, systematic
**Typography:** Mono or condensed sans (Space Grotesk, Barlow Condensed, IBM Plex Mono)
**Palette:** Concrete gray, warm white, utility yellow or safety orange
**Layout:** Structured grid, clear hierarchy, generous data density
**Motion:** Minimal — functional transitions only
**Good for:** B2B, tools, documentation, data-driven products

## Font Pairing Quick Reference

| Display Font | Body Font | Vibe |
|-------------|-----------|------|
| Playfair Display | Source Sans 3 | Classic editorial |
| Cormorant Garamond | Nunito | Organic luxury |
| Fraunces | DM Sans | Modern editorial |
| Syne | Inter (only if paired with bold display) | Tech-forward |
| Clash Display | Satoshi | Contemporary bold |
| Newsreader | Work Sans | Sophisticated content |
| Space Mono | Space Grotesk | Developer/tech |
| Outfit | Outfit (weight variation) | Clean versatile |
| Archivo Black | Archivo | Strong utilitarian |
| Comfortaa | Nunito Sans | Soft friendly |

**Rule:** If you've used a pairing in a recent project, pick a different one. Variety is essential.

## Color Composition Principles

1. **60-30-10 rule:** 60% dominant (background), 30% secondary, 10% accent
2. **Contrast is king:** Text must be readable. Use WebAIM contrast ratios.
3. **Temperature:** Warm palettes (reds, oranges, yellows) feel inviting. Cool palettes (blues, greens, purples) feel calm or professional.
4. **Unexpected accents:** A warm site with one cool accent color (or vice versa) creates visual interest
5. **Avoid safe defaults:** `#f5f5f5` background + `#333` text + blue links is the "I didn't design this" look
